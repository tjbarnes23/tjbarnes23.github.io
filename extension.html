8. Method Extension and Contraction

A.
Definitions
1.
Parent Method

A Method at a given Stage from which it may be possible to derive related Methods at higher or lower Stages using extension of contraction processes.

2.
Extension

A Method at a higher Stage than a Parent Method that is related to the Parent Method by an extension process.

3.
Contraction

A Method at a lower Stage than a Parent Method that is related to the Parent Method by a contraction process.

B.
General
1.

A Method extension (or contraction) process can be applied to the structure of a Parent Method at one Stage to produce the structure of related Methods at higher (or lower) Stages.

Further explanation: There are different extension and contraction processes. Not all Methods have a structure that is amenable to extension, and some Methods have a structure that is amenable to more than one extension process.

2.

The Central Council’s List of Extension Processes, with full definition and advice on their interpretation and application, is available at [ref TBD].

C.
Desirable Characteristics to Retain
1.

The extension relationship should cover an indefinite number of stages.

Further explanation: An extension process does not need to produce extensions at every higher stage, but it should not stop producing extensions before stage 32.

2.

The extension of a method should have the same symmetry as the parent.

[Need to define the symmetries to be preserved, e.g. palindromic and double.]

3.

The extension of a method should have the same number of hunt bells as the parent.

[Needs refining to cover extension by one stage by adding a hunt bell.]

4.

The extension of a method should have the same number of cycles of working bells as the parent. Where a method has two or more cycles of the same size, this characteristic should be retained in all extensions.

[To be discussed – some non-differential methods extend quite naturally to short-course.]

5.

The extension of a method should retain the same lead-end type.

Further explanation: There are 4 lead-end types: Plain Bob, Grandsire, Cyclic, and Other.

6.

The extension of a method should not have a bell making more or fewer consecutive blows than are made at the related position in the parent.

7.

Wherever the parent has a place made adjacent to the path of a hunt bell, this characteristic should be retained in all extensions.

8.

Wherever the parent has working bells making adjacent places, this characteristic should be retained in all extensions.
