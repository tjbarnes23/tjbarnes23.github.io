12. Transitional Arrangements

1.

Under this framework, the Classification of a small percentage of Methods in the Methods Library will change, and this will cause some Method Titles to Change.

By default, any Class Descriptor terms that previously applied, but which will no longer apply, will be incorporated in the Method Name.

E.g. for Shakespeare Tower Block Minor, the Method Name is Shakespeare Tower, the Class Descriptor is Block, and the Stage is Minor. Under the new framework the Class will change to "Surprise". Block will therefore be added to the Method Name such that the new Method Name will be Shakespeare Tower Block. The Method Title will therefore become Shakespeare Tower Block Surprise Minor.

The default approach above may result in naming conflicts for some Methods, in which case the Central Council will propose and publish new names.

If the band that named a method whose Classification will change under the new framework would like their Method named differently from the approach above, they are invited to submit the proposed new name to the T&T Workgroup.

2.

Any Peals as defined under this framework that were previously rung but which were not recognized as such under the Decisions in place at the time may be submitted to the T&T Workgroup for retroactive recognition. The Central Council will retroactively include these Peals in its analyses and records if it determines, based on good faith assessments of the available information about these Peals, that the requirements for a Peal under this framework were met.

If such retroactively recognized Peals were not previously published as Peals by The Ringing World, the T&T Workgroup will forward the details of these Peals to The Ringing World for publication, with the publication noting their retroactive recognition status.

If such retroactively recognized Peals were previously published as Peals by The Ringing World, the T&T Workgroup will ask The Ringing World to publish a notice stating that these Peals have now been included in the Central Council's analyses and records, and providing the references to the original publications by The Ringing World.

If a Peal was previously published by The Ringing World in a contrived manner that enabled it to be recognized as a Peal under the Decisions in place at the time, the band may resubmit the Peal to The Ringing World for republication in the form that the band originally intended. The republication will include a cross-reference to the original publication.

3.

The T&T Workgroup will add any new Methods that were rung in retroactively recognized Peals to the Method Library if these Methods have not already been added as a result of subsequent Performances.
If a Method was added to the Method Library as the result of a subsequence Performance and the original Performance of the Method has now been retroactively recognized, the reference in the Method Library to the first Performance of the Method will be updated accordingly.

4.

Sections 5.E and 9.A refer to the Central Council's Variations Library and Calls Library. These do not yet exist, but will be developed to provide central libraries for what today are collections maintained by individuals.

5.

On adoption of this framework, various changes will be required to the Method Library, and also to related software and other documentation. This may take some time.

6.

This framework calls for an analysis of Quarter Peals, which isn't performed today by the Central Council. This may take some time to implement.

7.

This framework is expected to change over time as Method Ringing continues to evolve. The Central Council maintains this framework and will periodically undertake transparent review of it to ensure it continues to meet the needs of the ringing community.
